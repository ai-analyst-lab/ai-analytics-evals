# Retention rate (prose definition)

This note explains what we mean by retention in plain language, for analysts and stakeholders who
want the reasoning rather than a field to cite. It carries the SAME meaning as the structured
contract and the executable form; only the format differs, which is the whole point of the format
experiment (prose is where paraphrase, and therefore drift, enters).

Retention is about whether first-time buyers come back. For a given month, take the customers who
made their first purchase that month, then ask how many of them made at least one more purchase
within the next ninety days. Retention is that returning share, cohorted by the month of first
purchase. It is not overall repeat volume, and it is not a lifetime repeat rate.

Only completed orders count: exclude cancelled and returned orders, and exclude internal and test
accounts. The window is ninety days, measured from each customer's first completed order.

Do not confuse this with the lifetime repeat-purchase rate (which has no time bound and is not what
"retention" asks), or with an active-in-both-halves-of-the-year coverage measure (which is a
coverage figure, not a cohort return rate).

There is no result value in this note, by design: the figure is computed from the data each run.
